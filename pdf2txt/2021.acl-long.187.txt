abstract.
in this digital age, online users expect person-alized content.
to cater to diverse group of au-diences across online platforms it is necessaryto generate multiple variants of same contentwith differing degree of characteristics (senti-ment, style, formality, etc.).
though text-styletransfer is a well explored related area, it fo-cuses on Ô¨Çipping the style attribute polarityinstead of regulating a Ô¨Åne-grained attributetransfer.
in this paper we propose a hierarchi-cal architecture for Ô¨Åner control over the at-tribute, preserving content using attribute dis-entanglement.
we demonstrate the effective-ness of the generative process for two differentattributes with varied complexity, namely sen-timent and formality.
with extensive experi-ments and human evaluation on Ô¨Åve real-worlddatasets, we show that the framework can gen-erate natural looking sentences with Ô¨Åner de-gree of control of intensity of a given attribute..1.introduction.
the ubiquity of online social networks and worldwide web has brought in diverse and often conÔ¨Çict-ing groups of users consuming similar informationbut from different perspectives.
so the onus falls onthe content producer to cater customized contentbased on the users‚Äô proÔ¨Åle.
consider an exam-ple related to a spanish football (soccer) league.
say the news is ‚Äúbarcelona has defeated realmadrid‚Äù.
this news needs to be presented in differ-ent tones to a barcelona fan - ‚Äúbarcelona smashedreal-madrid‚Äù, a real-madrid fan - ‚Äúreal madridlost the epic battle‚Äù and a (say) villarreal fan -‚Äúbarcelona wins three points against real-madrid‚Äù.
automatic generation of content with Ô¨Åne regu-lation of attributes like sentiment and style is ex-tremely beneÔ¨Åcial in this context.

several of the existing style-transfer methods (fuet al., 2018; john et al., 2018) convert a continu-ous entangled generative representation space ob-tained using variational auto-encoder (bowmanet al., 2015) into disentangled attribute and contentspace.
